# EKOS Interview Notes #1

## The Day EKOS's Core Idea Became Clear

**Date:** 2026-06-28

---

# Why This Interview Matters

Today's discussion was not simply interview practice.

It clarified the fundamental question behind EKOS.

Originally, EKOS was often described using implementation terms:

* Ontology
* Graph
* RAG
* SHACL
* Enterprise Knowledge Graph

During the interview it became clear that these are **implementation choices**, not the core idea.

The real question EKOS tries to solve is much simpler.

> **How can enterprise meaning survive while AI models continuously evolve?**

---

# The Core Insight

The most important realization today:

> **AI models evolve. Enterprise meaning endures.**

LLMs improve rapidly.

Context windows become larger.

Reasoning becomes stronger.

Token costs continue to fall.

But enterprise meaning does not disappear.

Relationships between business objects, process states, evidence, organizational rules, and execution boundaries continue to exist regardless of which model is being used.

EKOS exists to preserve that meaning.

---

# What EKOS Is NOT

EKOS is **not**

* a Graph project
* an Ontology project
* a better RAG
* another Agent framework

Those are implementation details.

EKOS is an attempt to preserve enterprise meaning in a durable form.

---

# The Most Important Interview Lesson

When asked

> "Why does EKOS still matter if future GPT models become nearly perfect?"

The answer should NOT focus on:

* larger context
* token savings
* better graphs

Instead:

A larger context window solves a **capacity** problem.

EKOS solves a **meaning preservation** problem.

Future models may become capable of reconstructing enterprise meaning from raw information every time.

EKOS preserves that meaning once so future AI systems can reason over it consistently.

Token savings are a consequence of that design.

They are not the primary objective.

---

# Why SAP?

Another important realization.

SAP is not the destination.

SAP is the proving ground.

SAP contains one of the world's richest enterprise knowledge environments:

* business objects
* execution history
* documents
* ABAP logic
* integrations
* policies
* organizational workflows

If EKOS can preserve enterprise meaning in SAP,

the same principles should generalize to many enterprise systems.

---

# What Makes EKOS Different

The difference is not that EKOS stores more information.

The difference is that EKOS attempts to preserve:

* business identity
* business relationships
* evidence
* process state
* execution boundaries
* organizational intent

Those are the pieces that AI systems repeatedly reconstruct today.

---

# The Biggest Personal Lesson

The strongest part of EKOS is not the implementation.

The strongest part is the problem definition.

Instead of asking

> "How can I build a better RAG?"

EKOS asks

> "Why do AI systems repeatedly lose enterprise meaning?"

That question defines the project.

Everything else follows from it.

---

# Interview Mindset Going Forward

Future interview preparation should not focus on memorizing answers.

Instead, every difficult question should strengthen EKOS itself.

The goal is not merely to pass interviews.

The goal is to make the project intellectually stronger after every discussion.

Every challenge,

every criticism,

every skeptical question

should improve EKOS.

---

# Guiding Principle

> **EKOS preserves enterprise meaning so future AI systems don't have to reconstruct it from scratch.**

This sentence currently represents the clearest description of the project.

---

# Personal Reminder

Do not become emotionally attached to implementation.

Be attached to the problem.

Graphs may change.

Ontologies may change.

Models will certainly change.

The problem remains.

Always optimize for preserving enterprise meaning--not for defending today's implementation.
